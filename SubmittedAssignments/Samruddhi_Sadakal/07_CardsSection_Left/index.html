Indian Festivals

The Country of joy and happiness

To experience the festivals of India is to experience the grandeur and richness of the Indian cultural heritage. The festivals of India thrive in a culture of diversity, and the celebration of these festivals has become a time for cross-cultural exchanges

Holi

Every spring, people across India and around the world celebrate the Hindu festival Holi, throwing colored water and powders on one another in joyous celebration.

Diwali

Diwali, one of the major religious festivals in Hinduism, Jainism, and Sikhism, lasting for five days from the 13th day of the dark half of the lunar month Ashvina to the second day of the light half of the lunar month Karttika.

Gudi Padwa

Gudi Padwa is a spring festival marking the start of the lunisolar new year for Marathi and Konkani Hindus. It is celebrated in and around Maharashtra, Goa & Damaon at the start of Chaitra, the first month of the lunisolar Hindu calendar.
